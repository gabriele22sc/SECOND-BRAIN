# Many, a lot of
- **Many** e **a few**: si usano con i sostantivi numerabili. Es: Were there many people at Mary’s? No, not many. - I’ve got a few pairs of shoes.
- Con sostantivi non numerabili si utilizza **much**(molto) e **a little**(poco). Es: Have you got much work to do? No, not much - There’s a little drive left. - Do you speak French? A little.
- **A lot of** ha un significato simile a much e many e si usa sia per i sostantivi numerabili sia per quelli non numerabili. Regola generale: si usa **a lot of** nelle frasi affermative e **much** e **many** in frasi negative e nelle domande. Es: I’ve got a lot of homework tonight. - We haven’t got much homework tonight.


# How much? e How many?
Le domande con **How much**(quanto/a) e **How many**(quanti/e) sono comuni e utili. 

Es: How much money have you got on you? - How many people were at the party?

# Few e Little
- In genere **a few** e **a little** si usano per esprimere un fatto in maniera neutrale(senza giudizio).

Es: There were <u>a few people</u> at the party. - There was <u>a little snow</u> last night.

- si usano **few** e **little**(senza articolo) per esprimere un’idea negativa, come “meno di quanto ci si aspettasse” o “meno di quanto si sperasse”.

Es: There were <u>few people</u> at the party. It was a bit boring. - There’s been <u>little snow</u> all year. You can’t ski.

# More, fewer e less
**More**(più) si usa sia con sostantivi numerabili che non numerabili.
**Fewer**(meno) si usa con sostantivi numerabili e **less**(meno) con sostantivi non numerabili.

Es: There are <u>more students</u> this year. 
There’s <u>more traffic</u> today.
There are <u>fewer students</u> this year.
There’s <u>less traffic</u> today.

# Only, just e very
Only, just e very possono essere usati per rafforzare l’idea di quantità piccole:
- Si usano **only** e **just** con **a few/a little**. Es: <u>Only a few</u> people went to the meeting. It was disappointing. - There was **only a little** rain this morning. - Do you speak italian? <u>Just a little</u>.
- Si usa **very** con **few/little**(senza a). <u>Very few</u> people passed the test. - There’s been <u>very little</u> snow all winter
